UX Reserch and Design: Nina van Vliet, Christa Rodriguez, Matthew Plumstead

Sage was created as a group project in the University of Minnisota UX/UI Bootcamp. We were given roughly 3 weeks to go from concept to mid fidelity prototype.

Development Stages
Problem to be Solved (current) Research Definition Prototype Take Aways

Problem:

Gardeners have an overwhelming number of resources for information, wich is time consuming and disorganized.

Solution:

We created a mobile app that takes into acccount our user’s unique circumstances an resents them with information and recommendations in one place.

1. Research

Quantitative

Google surveys are a great way to narrow down information and target user pain points. This is how we identified that gardeners have a confusing amout of sources.

Qualitative

I took charge of qualitative research. My goals were to:

1. Ask questions that let users tell me their stories.

2. Let these stories give me authentic unique insights

We learned gardeners trust friends and neighbors for the most relevant information.

We decided to make an app that recreated a neighbor telling you how to grow the most beautiful flowers in your garden.

Sample Questions

What feelings do you associate with Gardening?

What inspires your gardening decisions?

What things make you feel limited when gardening?

What are you processes like when you want to start planting?

2. Definition

Sage is unique because it offers “Sage Advice” when you select plants you want to grow. It can make suggestions based off of color, companion planting, size, etc.

We created a user flow that shows exactly how and when Sage will offer users advice.

3. Prototype

Sage became a soothing app to play with and a source of inspiration. We included a basic care instructions, built in reminders for watering and fertilizing.

Given more time we would build in a social media sharing platform for higher engagement and inspiration for users.

4. Take Aways

Properly scripted interviews are not only fun, but highly productive. Users’ insights can open paths you couldn’t find on your own.

I can fill a project management role. I enjoy encouraging creativity but know when to stop scope creep.

Having teammates you can trust to do the work is a relief that frees up energy that can be directed back into the work. I was lucky to work with fantastic people. Thanks Mac and Nina!
